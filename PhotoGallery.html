Our Videos

Winter Birding at Cherry Valley

Please join us for some tips on winter birding (including the birds you may encounter) at the Cherry Valley National Wildlife Refuge. Our wonderful guide for this video is Darryl Speicher of the Pocono Avian Research Center at the Pocono Heritage Land Trust; thank you Darryl!

Freshwater Mussel Snorkeling Survey in Cherry Creek at Cherry Valley

Youth from the Groundwork Hudson Valley Green Team assisted refuge staff by snorkeling in the Cherry Creek to conduct a visual survey for freshwater mussels at the Cherry Valley National Wildlife Refuge in summer of 2021.

Amphibians of Cherry Valley National Wildlife Refuge

Spring is a great time of year to visit the Cherry Valley National Wildlife Refuge to see and hear all kinds of amphibians. These underappreciated creatures are fascinating to watch, which you can do by checking out our video of some underwater footage of the eastern newt (Notophthalmus viridescens) taken in a pool on the Back Nine Trail at the Headquarters Tract (2138 Croasdale Road, Stroudsburg, PA 18360) of the Cherry Valley National Wildlife Refuge

Native Plants with the Brodhead Watershed Association

As part of the 2021 Friends of Cherry Valley National Wildlife Refuge Spring Fling, please enjoy our native plant video. Valuable information on why native plants are important and how to select them is provided by our partners from the Brodhead Watershed Association. Information is provided by BWA's Carol Hillestad, Michelle Riley, and Dr. Alex Jackson. This video was made in collaboration with the U.S. Fish & Wildlife Service.

Winter Tour of the Front Nine Trail at Cherry Valley

Please join us for a winter tour of the 1.7-mile Front Nine Trail at the Headquarters Tract (2138 Croasdale Road, Stroudsburg, PA 18360) of the Cherry Valley National Wildlife Refuge.
