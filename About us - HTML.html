Our History & Our Goals

Our History

Established in 2002, Friends of Cherry Valley is a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization located in Stroudsburg, Pennsylvania, formed by a passionate group of concerned residents and conservationists dedicated to preserving the natural beauty and ecological integrity of the Cherry Valley area.

Over the years, we have partnered with local governments, environmental agencies, and community organizations to promote conservation initiatives, organize educational programs, and host events that celebrate the natural resources of Cherry Valley. Through volunteer efforts and community involvement, we have successfully contributed to the establishment of protected lands and the restoration of critical habitats.

Today, Friends of Cherry Valley continues to serve as a vital voice for the community, fostering a deeper connection between residents and their natural surroundings while inspiring future generations to appreciate and protect the environment. Our commitment to conservation and land preservation remains strong as we look to the future, working towards a sustainable and thriving Cherry Valley for all.

Our Goals

  • Improve connections and communications with homeowners, landowners, and the community regarding conservation and land preservation.
  • Protect open space and environmentally sensitive areas.
  • Strengthen our Board to prepare future leaders.
  • Provide support to and community involvement in the Cherry Valley National Wildlife Refuge.
